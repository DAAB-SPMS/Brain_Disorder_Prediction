Alzheimer's disease (AD) is a progressive and irreversible neurological disorder that affects the brain, leading to memory loss, cognitive impairment, and changes in behavior and personality. The exact cause of Alzheimer's disease is not yet fully understood, but it is believed to be influenced by a combination of genetic, environmental, and lifestyle factors. The early symptoms of Alzheimer's disease may include mild memory loss, difficulty with problem-solving, and changes in mood or behavior. As the disease progresses, these symptoms become more severe, with individuals experiencing significant memory loss,difficulty communicating, and a loss of the ability to perform daily activities.
